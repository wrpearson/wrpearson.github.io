Identifying homologs and non-homologs; effects of scoring matrices and algorithms

1. Use the FASTA search page to compare Drosophila glutathione transferase gtt1_drome (gi|121694) to the PIR Annotated protein sequence database.

(a) What is the highest scoring non-homolog? (How would you confirm that your candidate non-homolog was truly unrelated?) What happens to the length of the alignment as non-homologous sequences are aligned?

(b) Note that this drosophila glutathione transferase shares significant similarity with both sequences from E. Coli and mammals. How might you confirm that the E. coli stringent starvation protein is homologous to glutathione transferases? (Hint - search SwissProt for a more comprehensive view of the family)

(c) Compare the expectation (E()) values for the distant relationships between gtt1_drome and xurt8c (SwissProt gta3_rat) or xurtg4 (SwissProt gta1_rat). How would you demonstrate that gtt1_drome is homologous to xurtg4? (Hint - You can use SwissProt to find intermediate sequences, but you don't need to)

(d) Examine how the expectation value changes with different scoring matrices (BLOSUM62, BlastP62, PAM250) and different gap penalties. (The default scoring matrix for the FASTA programs is BLOSUM50, with gap penalties of -10 to open a gap and -2 for each residue in the gap - e.g. -12 for the first residue in the gap using the old FASTA parameters.) What happens to the E()-value for the highest scoring unrelated sequence with the different matrices? Look at the distribution of scores and the E()-value of the highest scoring unrelated sequence when the gap-open/gap-ext penalties are small (-7/-1).

(e) (optional) Try the search with ktup=1 (What is ktup?). FASTA uses the ktup parameter to adjust the sensitivity and speed of the search. With ktup=2, FASTA looks for "pairs" of matched identical residues to find regions of similarity. ktup=1 looks for singly-aligned residues, and thus takes longer.

Comparing protein /translated DNA to DNA/DNA comparison

(b) Use TBLASTN to compare gtt1_drome (121694) to the Plant section of Genbank. What is the highest scoring plant homologue? 4. Using the Genbank DNA sequence encoding gtt1_drome (dmgst, gi|8033).

(a) Search the Arabidopsis protein sequences with BLASTX What are the E()-values for Arabidopsis GSTs?

(b) Copy the gi|8033 cDNA sequence into the BLASTX query sequence box.

(c) Put in 5 - 10 frame-shifts into dmgst gi|8033 (or use this sequence). Before submitting the search to BLAST, also copy it into a FASTX query window. Try the BLASTX search against arabidopsis proteins again.

Try the same search with the frame-shifted dmgst gi|8033 with FASTX. To compare with BLASTX, use the BlastP62 scoring matrix.

(d) Use BLASTN to compare dmgst (gi|8033) to the DNA sequences in Arabidopsis. Is there a detectable Arabidopsis homologue?

(e) optional The default BLAST DNA parameters are designed for very close matches (98% identity). Use the Other advanced options: -r +5 -q -4 -G 12 -E 4, which targets the DNA search for 70% identity.

(f) optional Use FASTA to compare dmgst to the Plant section of Genbank. Now what is the highest scoring Plant homologue?

(later)Try the search with ktup=3 rather than the default ktup=6.

5. Use prss to evaluate the statistical significance of a match. Note the difference between the p()-value and the E()-value. What would the conversion between p() and E() for a search of SwissProt?
