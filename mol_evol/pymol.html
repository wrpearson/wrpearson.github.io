BIMS6000 - Pymol for Dummies

1. Searching for sequences with known structure:

Use the FASTA site [pgm] or FASTADEMO site [pgm] (possibly with SSEARCH) to search the protein structure database (PDB Structures - NCBI) using your SNP target sequence (NP_ ...).

  1. Are there homologs with known structure to your protein? What is the E()-value of the closest homolog?

  2. Do any of the structures align to the full length of your human protein? How much of the protein is generally covered?

  3. (optional) What is the E()-value of the highest scoring protein that contains a different structural domain?

2. Look at the structural information:

Go to the PDB (Protein Databank) web site at pdb.org and look up one of the homologous protein structures you found. To lookup/download a structure, you need the PDB accession, which is the first 4 characters of the NNCBI PDB|chain accession. Thus, 1DX0A is really 1DX0.

Use the "Display files" option at the top-right hand of the page and display the PDB file header.

  1. What are the first 5 residues of the sequence based on the structure header?

  2. Does the structure start at residue 1?

  3. Do the numbers of the residues in the structural header match the number of the residues in the alignment?
    If not, you will need to remember the offset when you mark your SNP sites with Pymol.
3. Viewing with Pymol, highlighting SNPs:

Open your downloaded Pymol program.

  1. Using the fetch command in the Pymol command line box, fetch your structure from the PDB.
    Notice that the name of your structure on the highlighting box to the right of the viewing window.

  2. Use the S box to Show your structure as cartoons. Note that the lines stay.

  3. Use the H box to Hide the lines under the cartoons.

  4. Use the C box to Color your cartoon structure using secondary structure.

  5. In the command line box, create a new selection of one of the amino acids: 
      select snp1, resi 45
    The comma after snp1, is required. Use whatever amino-acid you like.)
    You should see that some number of atoms have been selected. Note that you have a new object called (snp1).

  6. Use the S box to Show those atoms as lines

  7. Use the C box to Color those atoms by element
